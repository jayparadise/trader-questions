O/U Trading Principles 

General Games

Formula for enhanced margin:

  • Win more on Overs and lose less on Unders

Three golden rules:

  1. Always try and identify if there is a sharp side of a bet (sharp users or a significant number of winning users)→ limit action on this side and refrain from offering inflated odds on it
  2. Protect Unders, especially if we have sharps & winning users betting on this side
  3. Avoid as much as possible offering inflated odds on Unders (i.e. 2.0+)

Others guidance points:

  • A sharp Under side (frequent) should be protected more aggressively than a sharp Over side (infrequent)
  • React to any initial action on Under from winning users… suggests this side is likely sharp
  • Conservatively trade casual exposure accumulating on Overs - i.e. don’t over react with aggressive price cuts
  • If Under price starts to inflate and results in winning users jumping on, react quickly and bring this Under price back down
  • Lower Over prices won’t necessarily detract casuals from betting Overs, so all we’re doing is inviting good users to take value on the Under at higher odds
  • We don’t want to be offsetting or hedging our Over exposure by letting winning users vacuum up Unders at inflated odds
  • Overall, this will result in more skewed setups where we lose more when Overs wins, but win more when Unders win... and our historic analysis shows Unders win more than Overs on player props
  • We don’t mind seeing inflated prices on Overs if we have a sharp Under side, but we  definitely need to avoid where possible offering inflated prices on Under (exception would be for v.low value stats that have small lines)
